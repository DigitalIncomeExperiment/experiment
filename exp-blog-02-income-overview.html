Nine income streams: what we set up and what happened

March 2026·All experiments·10 min read

Three months in. Four experiments confirmed earning. £58.84 confirmed across all streams. Here’s the full honest picture — every stream, what it involved, and what it has actually produced.

Why nine at once?

Most online income guides test one thing at a time. The Experiment runs all nine simultaneously for a specific reason: to compare real returns under the same conditions, in the same time period, with the same person behind all of them. No cherry-picking. No starting the clock when things got good. Everything from zero, everything at once.

The honest three-month verdict

What’s working: KDP is generating passive income — small but consistent. Surveys are easy money for low time investment. Games produced the biggest single monthly figure but requires active time and involves costs. Affiliate income is growing slowly but is real.

What isn’t working yet: Digital products need traffic to sell. Fiverr needs the first review to build momentum. Ko-fi needs an audience that feels invested enough to tip voluntarily.

The biggest result: Experiment 08 — the consumer rights claims. £1,500+ from a council tax challenge that cost nothing and took one phone call to convert to cash. The car finance DCA result is still pending but could exceed everything else combined. Neither of these is a “passive income stream” in the traditional sense — but they’re the most powerful reminder that sometimes the best income opportunity is money you’ve already overpaid.
