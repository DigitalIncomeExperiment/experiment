The story behind it

What is
the Experiment?

One person decided to stop wondering whether online income was real — and start finding out. This is that project.

The premise

The internet is full of people telling you how to make money online. Courses, YouTube channels, Reddit threads, Twitter threads — all promising that X method earns them thousands per month. But almost none of it comes with honest numbers, honest timelines, or any acknowledgement of what doesn't work.

This project started with a simple question: what actually happens when a normal person tries all of it at once? Not a full-time content creator. Not someone with an existing audience. Just someone starting from zero, documenting everything honestly.

Nine income streams were selected — a mix of passive, active, and speculative methods. All of them are running simultaneously. Every pound earned is reported. Every experiment that goes nowhere is reported equally.

Why nine streams at once?

Because testing one thing at a time would take years, and most people don't have years. Running everything in parallel lets us compare real returns side by side — not in theory, but in practice, in the same economic conditions, with the same time available.

It also means the results aren't cherry-picked. The good months and the flat months all get published. The methods that turn out to be a waste of time get the same column space as the ones that work.

What this is not

This is not a business. Nothing is sold on this site. There are no premium tiers, no paid reports, no coaching. Some links to tools and platforms are affiliate links — these are always disclosed and never influence what gets written. The only financial motive is testing whether the income methods themselves produce money.

The Experiment is not affiliated with any of the platforms being tested. Results documented here are one person's experience and should not be taken as typical or guaranteed.

Who runs it?

The Experiment is run by a private individual who prefers to keep their identity separate from the project — because this is about the data, not the personality. Content is produced and managed by Digital Revenue Studio, who handle the writing, site, and publishing — but do not own or direct the Experiment itself.

Think of it like a journalist embedded in a project they didn't commission. They document it faithfully, but the project belongs to someone else.
