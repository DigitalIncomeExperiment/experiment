What this means for your personal data

The Experiment is committed to being transparent — and that includes being clear about how subscriber data is handled right now, while DRS provides the infrastructure.

Your data — honest explanation

When you sign up on this site — for the free guide, the monthly income reports, or any other form — your email address is collected and held by Digital Revenue Studio on behalf of The Digital Income Experiment.

This is a temporary arrangement. DRS manages the Sender account that processes sign-ups and sends emails because the Experiment does not yet have its own independent email infrastructure. Your data is used only to deliver what you signed up for — the guide and/or monthly reports — and is passed on to the Experiment owner.

Your email will never be sold, shared for marketing, or added to any DRS mailing list without your separate consent. It belongs to the Experiment. DRS holds it in trust until the Experiment is self-sufficient.

Any replies to emails from the Experiment will go to [email protected] — that's DRS's address. All replies are forwarded to the Experiment owner. You're always talking to the Experiment, just via DRS's infrastructure for now.

For full details on how your data is handled, see the Privacy Policy. To unsubscribe at any time, use the link at the bottom of any email.

This arrangement is temporary — by design

The Experiment is called an experiment for a reason. Right now it's in its early phase — testing nine income streams from zero, documenting everything honestly, and reinvesting any income it generates into becoming more independent.

The goal is straightforward: as the Experiment earns income, it moves to its own infrastructure. Its own hosting. Its own Sender account. Its own email. At that point DRS steps back from operational support and the relationship becomes content-only — the same as any studio that produces work for a client and credits the client's ownership.

When the Experiment is self-sustaining, the following transitions:

Own domain and hosting • Own Sender account under the Experiment's own email • All subscriber data migrated from DRS's account to the Experiment's own • All email correspondence handled directly • DRS role becomes content production only, not infrastructure

Until then, every email from the Experiment will carry a note explaining this arrangement — so subscribers always know exactly where they stand.

One last thing

The Experiment's independence from DRS isn't just a legal nicety — it's the whole point. The findings, the income figures, the verdicts on what works and what doesn't — those belong to the Experiment's owner, not to DRS. DRS's job is to document it faithfully and build the platform around it. The data speaks for itself.

If you have any questions about this arrangement, about your data, or about anything on this site, you can reach us via DRS at [email protected] — your message will be passed on to the Experiment owner.
