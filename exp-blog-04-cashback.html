Quidco vs TopCashback — first real head-to-head result

March 2026·Experiment 05·5 min read

We needed a new hoover. Quidco offered 3.75% cashback through Currys. TopCashback offered 5.75%. The decision was easy — but what happened after clicking through was more interesting than expected.

The setup

Experiment 05 is running both major UK cashback platforms simultaneously — not one after the other, but at the same time — so when a qualifying purchase comes up, the rates can be compared directly and the better one chosen. The experiment documents every decision and every result honestly.

The first real test came when we needed a new hoover. A practical, necessary purchase — not manufactured for the sake of the experiment. The kind of spending that happens anyway, which is precisely when cashback earns its keep.

“The best cashback purchase is one you were already going to make. The cashback is pure addition — nothing was spent just to earn it.”

Both platforms checked before purchasing. The retailer: Currys. The category: floorcare. The rates at the time of purchase:

TopCashback was 2 percentage points higher for this category on this retailer. On a purchase of £189.99, that difference is meaningful — so TopCashback was used for this transaction.

What happened after clicking through

This is where cashback gets more nuanced than the headline rate suggests — and it's worth documenting honestly.

The purchase was completed at £189.99. TopCashback tracked the cashback almost immediately, which is a good sign. But the tracked value wasn't £189.99 — it was £158.33. And the cashback shown was £1.65, not the £10.92 that 5.75% of £189.99 would produce.

Why does the tracked value differ from the purchase price? Merchants often track cashback at a lower value than the purchase total — VAT, delivery charges, and certain product categories can be excluded depending on the retailer's cashback terms. The platform shows a message stating the merchant tracks at a lower rate, and that the value will be uplifted to the correct amount when the purchase is verified. This is normal — but worth knowing before you assume the headline rate applies to the full basket.

What to watch

The key question now: does the cashback uplift to the correct value on verification, as the platform states it will? The figure of £1.65 is on a tracked value of £158.33. If the full purchase price of £189.99 is verified, the cashback should be closer to the headline rate applied to that full amount.

This will be documented in the income report for the month it clears. Until then, £1.65 is what's tracked — and that's what gets reported here. Nothing gets counted until the money is confirmed.

Early lessons from Experiment 05

✓ Always check both platforms before any qualifying purchase

The rate difference on this transaction was 2 percentage points. On a £189.99 purchase that's a meaningful gap. Takes 60 seconds to check both. Always worth doing.

→ The tracked value is often lower than the purchase price

Don't assume the headline cashback rate applies to your full basket. Delivery, VAT, and specific product types can be excluded. Check the retailer's terms on each platform before purchasing if the amount matters.

· Fast tracking is a good sign, but not a guarantee

Immediate tracking is encouraging — it means the click-through registered and the platform knows a purchase happened. The actual cashback amount is confirmed later, after the retailer's return window closes. Patience required.

⚠ Watch out — first-purchase-only cashback
Many cashback offers are for new customers only. If you’ve ever used the retailer or service before, the cashback won’t pay out — even if it tracked. Always check the terms before completing a purchase. This caught us out on NordVPN — the cashback tracked, then was later declined because it wasn’t a first-time purchase. The golden rule: if you’ve used it before, assume the cashback won’t apply.
