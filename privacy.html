Privacy Policy

We collect very little data and we never sell it. This policy explains exactly what we collect, why, and what you can do about it.

Last updated: March 2026

The short version: We only collect your email address if you choose to sign up. We use it to send what you asked for. We never sell it, share it for marketing, or use it for anything you didn't agree to. You can unsubscribe or ask us to delete it any time.

01

Who We Are

The Digital Income Experiment is an independent project run by a private individual and documented by Digital Revenue Studio (DRS), a UK-based content studio.

For the purposes of data protection, the data controller for this site is Digital Revenue Studio. If you have any questions about how your data is handled, please contact us via digitalrevenuestudio.co.uk.

This site is hosted on GitHub Pages (GitHub, Inc., a subsidiary of Microsoft Corporation). GitHub Pages does not use cookies for tracking and does not serve ads.

02

What We Collect

We collect the absolute minimum required to operate this site and deliver what we've promised.

DataWhen collectedWhy
Email address When you sign up for the free guide or monthly updates To deliver the guide and/or send monthly income report emails
Basic analytics When you visit any page on this site To understand which content is useful (page views, referral sources). No personal identifiers stored.
IP address (server logs) Automatically, on every page visit Standard GitHub Pages server logging. Not used by us for tracking.

We do not collect names, addresses, payment information, or any sensitive personal data. We do not require an account to use this site.

03

How We Use Your Data

Your email address is used only for the following purposes, depending on what you signed up for:

  • Delivering "The First 90 Days" guide if you requested it
  • Sending the monthly income report email if you subscribed to updates
  • Sending occasional updates about significant new content or experiment milestones — no more than once a month

We do not use your email address to send promotional content on behalf of third parties. We do not share your email address with affiliates, partners, or advertisers. We do not use it for any purpose you did not explicitly consent to when you signed up.

We never sell your data. Ever, to anyone, under any circumstances.

04

Email Marketing — Sender

Email is sent via Sender, a third-party email marketing platform. When you submit your email address on this site, it is stored securely in Sender's systems.

Sender processes your data in accordance with their own Privacy Policy. They are GDPR compliant and do not sell subscriber data.

Standard email tracking features — such as open rates and click tracking — may be active on emails we send. This helps us understand whether our content is reaching people. If you prefer not to be tracked in this way, you can disable image loading in your email client, which prevents open tracking.

You can unsubscribe from any email at any time using the unsubscribe link at the bottom of every email we send. On unsubscribing, your email address will be removed from our mailing list promptly.

05

Cookies

This site does not use tracking cookies or advertising cookies of any kind.

GitHub Pages, which hosts this site, may set technical cookies necessary for the functioning of the service. These are not used by us for analytics or tracking purposes.

If we add analytics in future (such as a privacy-first tool like Plausible or Fathom), this policy will be updated before any such tool is activated.

06

This site interacts with the following third-party services. Each has its own privacy policy which governs how they handle data.

07

Your Rights

Under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

Right to access

You can ask what personal data we hold about you and receive a copy of it.

Right to erasure

You can ask us to delete your personal data. For email subscribers, unsubscribing handles this automatically.

Right to rectification

If any data we hold about you is inaccurate, you can ask us to correct it.

Right to object

You can object to us processing your data. For marketing emails, use the unsubscribe link in any email.

Right to portability

You can ask for your data in a structured, commonly used format.

Right to complain

If you're unhappy with how we handle your data, you can complain to the ICO (ico.org.uk).

To exercise any of these rights, contact us via Digital Revenue Studio. We will respond within 30 days.

08

Changes to This Policy

If we make material changes to this privacy policy — for example, if we add analytics tools, change email provider, or begin collecting additional data — we will update this page and revise the "last updated" date at the top.

We will not reduce your rights under this policy without your explicit consent.

09

Contact

For any questions about this privacy policy, to exercise your rights, or to request deletion of your data, please contact the content team via Digital Revenue Studio.

We aim to respond to all data-related requests within 30 days.
